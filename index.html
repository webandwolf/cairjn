Robles

We are a small production winery founded by Andy Neja and Robert Pierce. Decades of winemaking and farming in Paso Robles have given us a profound appreciation for the land, and its possibilities. Our wines explore traits and characteristics that are nurtured from the first point of inception in the vineyard.

As winemakers, we never want to make a wine that’s like another wine that we make. Each one is unique, with a unique perspective.

Our first bottling was a stainless steel aged Albariño that presents floral, citrus, and tropical aromas coupled with notes of green apple and stone fruit. This wine is the exact type of white I like to drink on a regular basis (hot or cold weather, doesn’t matter). It is the type of wine that you can share a bottle of while cooking dinner and not feel all boozy after a glass or two.

From there, we moved into a perfectly balanced Cabernet Sauvignon and Syrah blend, a 100% Syrah blend made up of four unique Willow Creek clones, a farmer-inspired Willow Creek Cabernet Sauvignon, a floral Grenache from Willow Creek, and a Rosé of Grenache that is fresh and vibrant with big citrus tones, strawberry, watermelon, and ocean mist.

There’s a lot we can say about our wines, but when it comes down to it the best way to understand what we’re about—what farming and winemaking in Paso Robles is about—is to come try them.
